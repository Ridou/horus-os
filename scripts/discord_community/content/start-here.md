# How this server works

A quick tour so you can find your way around.

## Reading channels (INFO)
- #welcome: rules, Code of Conduct, and key links.
- #announcements: official updates. You can follow this channel to mirror it into your own server.
- #releases: an automated feed of new Horus-OS releases from GitHub.

## Talking to people (COMMUNITY)
- #introductions, #general, and #off-topic for conversation.
- #showcase to post what you built. Standout builds get featured in #build-of-the-month.

## Getting help (SUPPORT)
- #help is a forum. Open a post with a clear title and the matching tag. Every question becomes its own searchable thread.
- #troubleshooting is for runtime and self-host failures, with logs and stack traces.
- #bug-reports is for confirmed defects only. Read the pinned template first.

## Agents and models
- #agents-runtime for autonomy, scheduling, and guardrails.
- #automations-and-crons for recurring tasks and recipes.
- #prompt-and-agent-recipes for shareable configs and profiles.
- #models-and-providers for Anthropic, Gemini, and local inference.
- #self-hosting for deployment, Docker, and OS-specific setup.

## Roles
Open "Channels and Roles" at the top of the channel list to self-assign:
- Notifications: get pinged for releases and announcements.
- Interests: agents, voice, dashboard, self-hosting, local models.
- Help: flag yourself as someone who can help, or as someone looking for help.

Contributor and Maintainer roles are granted by staff, not self-assigned, so they reflect real status.
